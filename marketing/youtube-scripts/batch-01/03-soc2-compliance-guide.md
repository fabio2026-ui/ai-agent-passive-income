## 📋 CONTENT STRUCTURE

### Section 1: What is SOC 2? (0:45 - 2:30)

**Definition:**
SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2) is an auditing framework developed by the AICPA that evaluates how service organizations handle customer data based on five Trust Services Criteria.

**The Five Trust Services Criteria (TSC):**

1. **Security (Required)**
   - Protection against unauthorized access
   - System is protected against unauthorized access

2. **Availability**
   - System is available for operation and use
   - Uptime commitments, disaster recovery

3. **Processing Integrity**
   - System processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely
   - Important for fintech, payment processors

4. **Confidentiality**
   - Information designated as confidential is protected
   - NDAs, encryption, access controls

5. **Privacy**
   - Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed, and disposed of properly
   - GDPR, CCPA alignment

**Visual:** Show the Trust Services Criteria as a pyramid with Security at the base

### Section 2: SOC 2 Type I vs Type II (2:30 - 4:00)

**Type I (Point-in-Time):**
- **Snapshot:** Assesses controls at a specific moment
- **Faster:** 2-4 months to complete
- **Cheaper:** $10,000-$30,000
- **Use Case:** First compliance milestone, early-stage startups
- **Question Answered:** "Are your controls designed appropriately?"

**Type II (Period of Time):**
- **Movie:** Tests controls over 3-12 months
- **Longer:** 6-12 months minimum
- **More Expensive:** $20,000-$80,000+
- **Use Case:** Enterprise requirement, gold standard
- **Question Answered:** "Do your controls actually work over time?"

**Comparison Table:**
| Aspect | Type I | Type II |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Timeline | 2-4 months | 6-12 months |
| Observation Period | None | 3-12 months |
| Cost | $10K-$30K | $20K-$80K+ |
| Enterprise Acceptance | Limited | Full |
| Audit Frequency | Annual | Annual |

**Recommendation:**
- Start with Type I if you're pre-revenue or seed stage
- Upgrade to Type II when you have enterprise customers asking for it

### Section 3: The SOC 2 Journey - Step by Step (4:00 - 7:30)

**Phase 1: Readiness Assessment (Week 1-2)**

**Gap Analysis:**
- Review existing policies and procedures
- Identify missing controls
- Assess current vs. required state

**Common Gaps Found:**
- No formal security policies
- Missing access review processes
- No incident response plan
- Lack of change management
- Insufficient logging/monitoring

**Tools:**
- Vanta, Drata, Secureframe, Hyperproof
- Compliance automation platforms

**Phase 2: Remediation (Week 3-8)**

**Control Implementation:**

1. **Access Control**
   - Role-based access control (RBAC)
   - Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
   - Quarterly access reviews
   - Offboarding automation

2. **System Operations**
   - Change management process
   - System monitoring
   - Backup and recovery procedures
   - Patch management

3. **Risk Management**
   - Risk assessment documentation
   - Vendor risk management
   - Business continuity planning
   - Incident response procedures

4. **HR & Training**
   - Background checks
   - Security awareness training
   - Confidentiality agreements
   - Acceptable use policies

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Information Security Policy
- Access Control Policy
- Change Management Policy
- Incident Response Plan
- Business Continuity Plan
- Vendor Management Policy
- Acceptable Use Policy
- Data Classification Policy

**Phase 3: Evidence Collection (Week 9-12)**

**What Auditors Want:**
- Screenshots of system configurations
- Policy documents (signed and dated)
- Training completion records
- Access review logs
- Penetration test reports
- Vendor security assessments
- Incident tickets (if any)

**Evidence Organization:**
- Use a compliance platform or shared drive
- Tag evidence by TSC criteria
- Maintain version control
- Include timestamps and context

**Phase 4: Audit (Week 13-16)**

**The Audit Process:**
1. Auditor reviews documentation
2. Walkthrough meetings with team
3. Sample testing (usually 25-50 samples)
4. Draft report review
5. Final report issuance

**Audit Days:**
- Type I: 1-2 days
- Type II: 2-5 days

**Common Auditor Questions:**
- "Show me how you onboard employees"
- "Walk me through your change process"
- "How do you know your backups work?"
- "What happens when you detect an incident?"

### Section 6: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them (10:00 - 11:00)

**Mistake #1: Treating It as a Checkbox Exercise**
- Reality: Auditors can tell when controls are "paper only"
- Solution: Actually implement what you document

**Mistake #2: Not Assigning Ownership**
- Reality: Compliance without an owner fails
- Solution: Designate a Compliance Lead (even if part-time)

**Mistake #3: Over-Engineering Controls**
- Reality: Startup controls should match startup size
- Solution: Right-size controls to your risk profile

**Mistake #4: Ignoring Vendor Compliance**
- Reality: You're responsible for your vendors
- Solution: Document vendor assessments

**Mistake #5: Waiting Too Long**
- Reality: SOC 2 takes months, deals close in weeks
- Solution: Start at least 6 months before you need it
