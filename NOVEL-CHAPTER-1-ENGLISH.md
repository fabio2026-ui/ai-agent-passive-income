# Chapter 1: Restaurant Day

It was supposed to be an ordinary Tuesday. I woke up in my tiny Milan apartment, the morning sun streaming through worn curtains, already dreading the day ahead.

Three years in Italy. Three years of grinding through culinary school, working every odd job I could find, saving every euro for this moment.

Today was Restaurant Day.

I'd signed the lease on a small space near Porta Venezia six months ago. Converted it from a failing gelato shop. Slept on the floor more nights than I care to admit while renovating.

Now, staring at my reflection in the bathroom mirror, I barely recognized myself. The dark circles under my eyes. The permanent flour stains on my hands. The desperate hope in my eyes that this wouldn't be another failure.

"Today's the day," I told my reflection. "Make it count."

I grabbed my jacket and stepped out into the Milan morning, the city already buzzing with life. Vespas zipping past. Fashionably dressed Italians heading to their jobs. Tourists consulting maps.

And me, walking toward my dream with €237.50 in my bank account and everything on the line.

The restaurant—*my* restaurant—sat on a quiet corner, its windows still covered in paper from the renovations. I'd named it "East & West" because that was my story. Chinese heritage, Italian training, hoping to bring them together on every plate.

I unlocked the door and stepped inside, inhaling the smell of fresh paint and possibility.

That's when I heard it.

**[DING!]**

A sound like crystal bells, right in my ear. I spun around, heart hammering, but the restaurant was empty.

**[DING!]**

**[System Activation: Divine Restaurant System binding to host...]**

I froze. The voice was in my head, clear as day, but no one else was here. Had I finally snapped? Too many sleepless nights, too much stress?

**[Binding successful!]**

**[Welcome, Host!]**

A translucent blue panel appeared before my eyes, floating in mid-air like something from a sci-fi movie. I stumbled backward, nearly knocking over a chair.

**[Divine Restaurant System]**
**[Host: Lin Mo]**
**[Restaurant: East & West]**
**[Level: 1]**

**[Newbie Mission: Earn €1,000 on Opening Day]**
**[Reward: Master-Level Pasta Recipe]**
**[Failure: All savings will be deducted]**

I stared at the panel, blinking hard. It didn't disappear. I pinched my arm. Ow. Not dreaming.

"This... this can't be real," I whispered.

**[The System is very real, Host!]**

**[I am here to help you become the greatest restaurant owner in the world!]**

**[But first, you must complete your newbie mission!]**

€1,000. In one day. With €237 in my account and no customers yet.

"That's impossible," I said out loud. "No restaurant makes €1,000 on opening day."

**[With the System, nothing is impossible!]**

**[But I should warn you... failure has consequences.]**

The panel highlighted the last line again: **[Failure: All savings will be deducted]**

All €237.50. Gone. I'd be broke, in debt, my dream dead before it started.

"No pressure then," I muttered, running my hands through my hair. "Just €1,000. In one day. With no customers, no marketing, no—"

I stopped. Looked around my empty restaurant. The gleaming kitchen I'd spent weeks cleaning. The tables I'd refinished by hand. The menu I'd designed, blending Chinese and Italian flavors that no one else in Milan was doing.

Then I looked at the System panel again.

**[Would you like to view your first reward?]**

I touched the panel instinctively, and it changed:

**[Master-Level Pasta Recipe: Carbonara]**
**[Description: Authentic Roman carbonara, elevated with subtle Eastern techniques. Guaranteed to impress even Michelin inspectors.]**
**[Success Rate: 100% when prepared according to System instructions]**

100% success rate. Authentic carbonara—every Italian's pride and joy—made better with techniques I knew from my grandmother's kitchen.

My hands started to shake, but not from fear. From excitement.

Maybe... just maybe...

"Alright, System," I said, rolling up my sleeves. "Let's make some money."

I headed into the kitchen, flipped on the lights, and fired up the burners. The System panel followed me, hovering in the corner of my vision like a helpful ghost.

**[First step: Prepare the carbonara using System recipe. Sell at premium price. Impress first customers. Build momentum.]**

Simple enough. Except I didn't have customers. Or a reputation. Or any idea how to get €1,000 in one day.

But I had something else.

I had fire in my belly, skill in my hands, and now—apparently—a divine system guiding my way.

I grabbed fresh eggs from the refrigerator, pecorino romano from my supplier, guanciale I'd cured myself over the past month. The pasta machine I'd imported from Bologna sat gleaming on the counter.

"East & West," I said to the empty restaurant. "Let's show Milan what we can do."

The System chimed happily in my ear.

**[That's the spirit, Host!]**

**[Now cook like your life depends on it!]**

**[Because financially... it kind of does!]**

I laughed despite myself. A system with a sense of humor. Great.

But as I started cracking eggs and mixing cheese, something shifted inside me. The fear didn't disappear, but it transformed. Became fuel.

I'd spent three years preparing for this moment. Studying under demanding Italian chefs who threw pans when dishes weren't perfect. Working double shifts until my feet bled. Saving every cent while living in a shoebox.

Now, on the first day of the rest of my life, the universe had given me a cheat code.

I wasn't going to waste it.

The pasta machine hummed to life. Fresh fettuccine emerged, perfect strands of golden dough. The guanciale sizzled in the pan, rendering its fat into liquid gold. The sauce came together in the bowl—eggs, cheese, pepper—waiting for the hot pasta to transform it into silk.

I'd made carbonara hundreds of times. But this time, following the System's subtle guidance—the exact timing, the precise temperature, the way to fold the pasta—I knew this one was different.

This one was magic.

I plated it simply. No garnish, no fuss. Just perfect pasta, glistening with sauce, pieces of crispy guanciale scattered on top.

"€25," I said, setting the price. Premium for opening day, but worth every cent.

Now I just needed someone to buy it.

I walked to the front door, peeled back the paper covering the windows, and flipped the sign to OPEN.

Outside, Milan continued its busy morning. People walked past, glancing at the new restaurant but not stopping.

Three minutes passed. Five. Ten.

My heart sank. Maybe this was a mistake. Maybe €1,000 in one day was as impossible as it sounded.

Then the door opened.

**[Chapter 1 End]**
